Problem 1: Socioeconomic Class Dataset

Introduction

We analyzed the Socioeconomic Class Data, student.csv, which contained the following columns:

Column 1: gender: With values female and male.

Column 2: schtyp: School type, with values public, private.

Column 3: read: The reading score of a standardized test.

Column 4: write: The writing score of a standardized test.

Column 5: math: The math score of a standardized test.

Column 6: science: The science score of a standardized test.

Column 7: honors: Whether or not the student is enrolled in an honors class, with values no, yes.

Column 8: ses: Socioeconomic classs

Analysis

We used an AIC, backward-forward selection because we want to do prediction later. We chose this type of selection because backward selection overfits, but backward-forward overfits a little bit less and we chose it AIC because it penalizes less than BIC.

Based on the criterion, our best fit model is: ses ~ gender + schtyp + read + honors with an AIC of 403.7529. Below are the estimated coefficents for the selected variables:

Low vs high: \(log_{IT}(\pi) = 2.644419 + (-0.8675304)X^{\text{gender}}_{1} + 1.6367326X^{\text{schtyp}}_{2} + (-0.07403496)X^{\text{read}}_{3} + (-0.2937044)X^{\text{honors}}_{4}\)

There is only two models because it is redundent to have the third model since we can solve for third model, low vs middle, by subtracting middle vs high from low vs high.

Problem 2: Baby Dataset

Introduction

Our goal is to use logistic regression and model selection so we can make a prediction of a women weighing 157 pounds, age 29, with no history of smoking, pre-mature labor, or hypertension, and 10 visits will have a low birth weight child. This is interesting problem because it allows us to see what variables go into having a healthy birth.

From our data baby.csv, we have the following columns:

Column 1: age: The age in years of the subject

Column 2: weight: The weight in pounds of the mother (before pregnancy).

Column 3: smoke: Smoking status during pregnancy, with levels yes and no.

Column 4: pre: History of pre-mature labor, with levels yes and no.

Column 5: hyp: History of hypertension, with levels yes and no.

Column 6: visits: The number of visits during their first trimester (first 3 months).

Column 7: birth: If the birth weight of the infant was low, or not. Your response variable for this problem, with values 1 (low birth weight), 0 (not low birth weight).

Summary

Given a baby is born with a low birth weight, pregnant women who do not smoke and do not have hypertension, are less likely to have a history of pre-mature labor. Given a baby is born with a low birth weight, pregnant women who do not smoke and have hypertension are less likely to have a history of pre-mature labor

Given that a baby was not born with a low birth weight, pregnant women who do not smoke and do not have hypertension, are less likely to have a history of pre-mature labor. However, if a pregnant women does have hypertension and does not smoke, there is a higher chance that they will have a history of pre-mature labor.

Mothers who have a child with low birth weight are generally between the ages of 15 to 40. The mother usually weighs about 90 to 200 pounds. The mother would generally get about 1 to 2 visitors.

Mothers who have a child that is not low birth weight are generally between the ages of 10 to 35. The mother usually weighs between 80 to 200 pounds. The most would generally get about 1 to 4 visitors.

Analysis

Since we want to do a prediction, we chose to use backward-foward AIC because AIC does not penalize as much as BIC. Backward selection results in an overfit model and more variables in your model leads to a better prediction. We chose to do backward-forward selection instead of simply backward selection because it will help us get closer to the best model.

Using backward-forward selection with AIC, our best model is: low ~ age + weight + smoke + pre + hyp with an AIC of 214.19.

Diagnostics

From the histogram plot above, we can see that not all of our residuals are normal. We can see that some residuals are smaller than -3 and they may be outliers/influential points.

We calcualted the change in Pearson Chi-Squared for each observation. When the change is large, it generally means that the observation we added may be an outlier or an influential point. We found five of changes in Pearsons Chi-Squared that were larger than 6, our cutoff value:

Conclusion

We found that the the variables that most influence a low birth weight are the mothers age and weight, whether or not she smoked during the pregnancy, her history of pre-mature labor, and history of hypertension. Our model fits the data well, but it does not nescessarily predict well.
